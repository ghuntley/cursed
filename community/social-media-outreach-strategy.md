# CURSED Social Media & Developer Outreach Strategy

## Platform Strategy Overview

### Primary Platforms

#### Twitter/X (@cursedlang)
**Target Audience:** Tech influencers, developers, early adopters  
**Content Focus:** Quick updates, code snippets, community highlights  
**Posting Frequency:** 3-5 times per day  

**Content Mix:**
- 30% Technical tips and code examples
- 25% Community spotlights and achievements
- 20% Industry discussions and trends
- 15% Event announcements and updates
- 10% Behind-the-scenes development content

#### LinkedIn (CURSED Programming Language)
**Target Audience:** Enterprise developers, CTOs, tech leaders  
**Content Focus:** Professional insights, case studies, thought leadership  
**Posting Frequency:** 1-2 times per day  

**Content Mix:**
- 35% Technical articles and deep dives
- 25% Industry analysis and comparisons
- 20% Success stories and case studies
- 15% Team updates and company news
- 5% Event and workshop announcements

#### YouTube (CURSED Code Channel)
**Target Audience:** Developers seeking tutorials and demos  
**Content Focus:** Educational content, live streams, project showcases  
**Upload Frequency:** 2-3 videos per week  

**Content Types:**
- Tutorial series (40%)
- Live coding sessions (25%)
- Community project showcases (20%)
- Developer interviews (10%)
- Conference talks and presentations (5%)

### Secondary Platforms

#### Reddit (r/cursedlang)
**Community Management:** Daily engagement and support  
**Content Focus:** Technical discussions, Q&A, project sharing

#### Discord (Primary Community Hub)
**Active Moderation:** 24/7 coverage with bot assistance  
**Content Focus:** Real-time support, collaboration, events

#### GitHub Discussions
**Developer Focus:** Technical RFC discussions, roadmap planning  
**Moderation:** Core team and senior contributors

## Influencer & Developer Relations

### Target Developer Communities

#### High-Priority Targets
- **Rust Developers** - Language feature similarities, performance focus
- **Go Developers** - Concurrency model, simplicity philosophy  
- **JavaScript/TypeScript Developers** - Modern syntax, tooling expectations
- **Systems Programmers** - Performance, safety, and control focus
- **Game Developers** - Real-time programming, creative expression

#### Engagement Strategy
1. **Identify Key Influencers** - Track 50+ developers in each community
2. **Value-First Approach** - Share helpful content before promotion
3. **Collaborative Content** - Joint tutorials, comparisons, interviews
4. **Cross-Pollination** - Share their content, engage genuinely
5. **Event Participation** - Sponsor, speak at, or attend their events

### Developer Advocate Program

#### Tier 1: Core Advocates (3-5 people)
- Full-time developer relations focus
- Conference speaking budget and support
- Direct line to engineering team
- Content creation resources and support

#### Tier 2: Community Champions (15-20 people)
- Part-time advocacy with recognition
- Event speaking opportunities
- Early access to features and content
- Community leadership responsibilities

#### Tier 3: Active Contributors (50+ people)
- Regular contribution recognition
- Social media amplification support
- Access to exclusive community channels
- Collaboration opportunities

### Conference & Event Strategy

#### Tier 2 Events (Selective Participation)

### Crisis Communication Plan

#### Issue Response Framework
1. **Acknowledgment** (Within 2 hours) - Recognize the issue publicly
2. **Investigation** (Within 24 hours) - Provide initial findings
3. **Resolution** (Target <72 hours) - Fix implementation and communication
4. **Follow-up** (1 week later) - Lessons learned and prevention measures

#### Communication Channels Priority
1. **Discord** - Real-time community updates
2. **Twitter** - Broader developer community
3. **Blog** - Detailed technical explanations
4. **GitHub** - Issue tracking and resolution

## Crisis Management & Reputation

### Monitoring & Early Warning
- **Brand mention tracking** across all platforms
- **Sentiment analysis** of community discussions  
- **Issue escalation alerts** from support channels
- **Competitor activity monitoring** for context

### Response Protocols
- **Positive Amplification** - Share and celebrate good mentions
- **Neutral Clarification** - Provide helpful information and context
- **Negative Engagement** - Address concerns transparently and professionally
- **Crisis Escalation** - Coordinate with legal/leadership for serious issues
